    The problems of censorship in Singapore today are more complex than a decade ago, Mass travel has made it very difficult for a society to filter foreign influences. Just as bad money drives out good money, debased values and bizarre life-styles spread from country to country, undermining and eroding established virtues and values. All these have changed the problems of censorship. Technological advances, for example, have brought about the invention of video tapes and video discs which make it possible for films hitherto viewed only in cinema halls to be available for private viewing.     The Bill also enhances the penalties for the exhibition, distribution and possession of uncensored and pornographic tapes. The penalties are on per copy basis. For exhibition and distribution, there is also provision for an additional penalty of an imprisonment term not exceeding six months. The penalties are heavier for the possession, exhibition and distribution of pornographic tapes. Under clause 29, the penalties for possession of pornographic tapes are a fine of not less than $500 per copy but not exceeding the aggregate of $20,000 or an imprisonment term not exceeding six months or both fine and imprisonment.
